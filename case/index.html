01 · The Work

Eleven films
grown from spoken worlds

We asked blind and low-vision friends to describe their worlds, then translated those descriptions into moving images. In the room the films are the size of a hand; here, they play at your size.

01
The Crane and the Moon
Someone described the moon as something near — near enough to install. So we hung it.
02
The Threshold
Half of it is not yet said; half already understood. The border keeps moving, and dust crosses through the light.
03
Brightness That Refuses
That brightness is not for seeing — it forces the eyes shut. Only when he said "bright" did we learn that brightness can refuse.
04
A Cathedral Overheard
A building never visited, built from adjectives overheard. The grain of the stone is the residue of other people's voices.
05
Steles in Fog
Grass to the knee, steles in fog. A shape without a name can still be remembered — remembering how it feels is enough.
06
Assemblage at Dusk
A column, pipes, a cracked tub: sentences from different people piled into one dusk. None is whole; together they resemble a home.
07
The Wire Sphere
The thing inside can't be explained — only that it burns, and that wire wraps around it. The fire is small; it stays lit.
08
A Planet on No Map
The world he spoke of is on no map. Fine — give it a glowing horizon and a few yellow moons. It is wherever you say it is.
09
The Doorway
She stands in the doorway; beyond it, a sky described to her a thousand times. Seeing was never something we could finish on her behalf.
10
Two Cats
Two cats on a white city, watching the sun go down. Neither explains orange to the other. Some company precedes understanding, and asks for none.
11
Daytime Through Paper
The whole world gauzed in yellow, like light seen through paper. He said: this is my daytime. So we placed the camera inside the paper, too.

The captions are the translators' second readings — not quotations. Some films combine the descriptions of several storytellers. They record how we listened, not what was said.

02 · Eight Voices

Eight channels fill the dark from eight corners; they never pair with the screens. To see, you walk to an image; to listen, you stand where the channels balance.

Combined stereo reference, 11'09". In the room they play as eight independent sources. The sound layer is complete in itself: for blind and low-vision visitors it is the entire work.

03 · Research

Everyone lives on second-hand experience —
and the handover always leaks

Field research (short interviews N=15 · bilingual micro-survey N=23, July 2026) drawn one person at a time, beside representative surveys and controlled experiments that find the same condition.

82%
of U.S. adults at least sometimes read online reviews before buying something new.
Pew Research Center, Online Reviews, 2016 · N=4,787
72%
of internet users looked online for health information — other people's illness, told as experience.
Pew Research Center, Health Online 2013
34%
of U.S. adults have used ChatGPT — experience now arrives second-hand through a model as well.
Pew Research Center, June 2025 · N=5,123
≈
Couples communicate no more accurately than strangers — but are far more confident that they do.
Savitsky, Keysar, Epley, Carter & Swanson, The closeness-communication bias, JESP, 2011
×25
Across 25 experiments, imagining another's perspective did not increase accuracy. What worked was getting it: asking, listening, conversing.
Eyal, Steffel & Epley, Perspective Mistaking, JPSP, 2018
2.2B
2.2 billion people live with vision impairment; a million blind users are connected to ten million volunteers who describe the world by live video.
WHO, 2019 · Be My Eyes, 2026
"Sweet like sugar — but the small part that isn't sugar is usually the part that matters." P13 · the good-enough theory of understanding, from the field

Field data is a small qualitative sample; the surveys and experiments are representative or controlled. Shown side by side, never interchanged.

04 · Appendix — Taking It to a Room

Four halls, one light script

A proposed staging at Warehouse XI: a dim buffer, a blackout star field of fifteen hand-height screens, a low-lit echo hall with a braille sentence wall and a card taken in the dark, a bright lounge where the card is finally read.

1 · BUFFERdim
2 · STAR FIELDblackout
3 · ECHOlow
